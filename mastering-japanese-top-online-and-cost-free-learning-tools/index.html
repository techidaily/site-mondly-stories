How to learn Japanese by yourself: Tips and Tricks

How to learn Japanese on your own for free? Learning Japanese for beginners is not necessarily an easy task, but we can help you by providing some tips and tricks you can use. Learn how to speak Japanese by using the following steps:

  • Learn to read Hiragana. The Japanese language actually uses an alphabet made up of three scripts: hiragana, katakana and kanji. Start by learning how to read hiragana. You’ll find a lot of resources online, some even with illustrations!
  • Then, approach Katakana. Even the types of pen and paper are important so pay attention;
  • Become familiar with Kanji and remember to practice it constantly;
  • Start building a core vocabulary. We’ll give you some resources, for example, Japanese words , phrases and expressions , greetings and counting ;
  • Learn the basics of Japanese pronunciation. Get your hands on a good pronunciation dictionary or use the Mondly learning method . Find out more about our Japanese lessons !
  • Approach Japanese grammar by getting familiar with the basic particles;
  • Understand the Japanese sentence structure. The basic ones are “Subject Object Verb.”

How long does it take to learn Japanese on your own?

Well, as we already mentioned, the FSI claims that on average you’ll require about 2200 hours or 88 weeks of study to become fluent in Japanese. However, depending on your resources and motivation, but also your goals, it may take even approximately 90 days to learn basic level Japanese.

So, you can invest 30 minutes to an hour every day and you’ll be conversational in Japanese in, give or take, 3 months. But, is it possible to learn Japanese on your own? Yes, very much so, given the right resources!

Did you know that the Japanese language has no grammatical gender and no articles? Using the right course, you’ll soon find out a lot more “learning Japanese” tips that will make you feel confident about your journey towards Japanese fluency.

Since we referenced grammar, there are quite a few important things to mention:

  • As we said, nouns have no gender;
  • Most nouns don’t have separate plural forms;
  • The subject is optional and you may omit it;
  • The predicate will always be at the end of the sentence;
  • The verbs won’t change according to the subject or number;
  • Personal pronouns will differ according to the level of formality of the situation.
