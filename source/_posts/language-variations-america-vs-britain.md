### Collective nouns

 In American English, collective nouns such as ‚Äúband‚Äù, ‚Äúteam‚Äù, ‚Äúfamily‚Äù or ‚Äúcrew‚Äù are considered plural. In contrast, British English considers collective nouns either singular or plural, but more often plural as a way to emphasize the members of the group.

### Learned or learnt?

**Learned** and**learnt** are both the correct past participle and past tense forms of the verb ‚Äúto learn‚Äù. Easily overlooked in speech, but much more obvious in writing, this subtle difference addresses verbs.

 In the UK, the past participle of ‚Äúget‚Äù ‚Äì ‚Äúgotten‚Äù ‚Äì is considered archaic and was replaced with ‚Äúgot‚Äù some time ago. Americans, on the other hand, still use ‚Äúgotten‚Äù as the past participle of ‚Äúget‚Äù.

**American English:** get ‚Äì got ‚Äì gotten
