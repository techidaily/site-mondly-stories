## Understanding the process: the cognitive advantages of kids

 All around this whole wide world, there are many kids who learn two languages at the same time starting from the first or second year of their life.  On the other hand, when you study for a test, retaining information is not nearly as easy. And that happens because **you make a conscious effort to learn ‚Äì a process that makes it more difficult for your brain to gather information.**

 If you want to dive into it and find some scientific research to back this up, you‚Äôll probably discover Dr. Paul Thompson‚Äôs [study](http://users.loni.usc.edu/~thompson/MEDIA/press%5Frelease.html) at UCLA. This is why they pick up words and phrases without much effort ‚Äì the same we do with songs. The conclusion of the study was, unsurprisingly, that language acquisition is second nature to children.

 Furthermore, as we already discussed, **the brain‚Äôs plasticity and structure change completely by the time we reach puberty.** We become more skilled at complex thought and intellectual learning and we begin to process information differently. Our brain doesn‚Äôt focus on language learning as much as it did a few years back.
