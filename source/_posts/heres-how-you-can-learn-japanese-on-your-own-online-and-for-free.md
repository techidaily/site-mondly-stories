* Learn to read Hiragana. Start by learning how to read hiragana. You’ll find a lot of resources online, some even with illustrations!
* Then, approach Katakana. Even the types of pen and paper are important so pay attention;
* Become familiar with Kanji and remember to practice it constantly;
* Start building a core vocabulary. ## How to learn Japanese on your own?

 Since we referenced grammar, there are quite a few important things to mention:

* As we said, nouns have no gender;
* Most nouns don’t have separate plural forms;
* The subject is optional and you may omit it;
* The predicate will always be at the end of the sentence;
* The verbs won’t change according to the subject or number;
* Personal pronouns will differ according to the level of formality of the situation.
